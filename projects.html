The UAC Showcase was a variety show with 14 different student organizations, ranging from A Capella, light shows, dance, theatre, comedy, quiz bowl competitions, and games. Each student organization performed two sets. I organized marketing for the show, bringing in over 250 attendees to the free event in the Lydia Mendelssohn Theatre at the University of Michigan. I managed the $2,000 budget for the event, and secured sponsorship with creative marketing opportunities mid-event. Throughout this event I managed several stakeholders, including all 14 student organizations, the University’s Center for Campus Involvement, The Tech Shop, and Apple.



Over the past 3 years, I have been a part of 5 different shows with Big Ticket Productions, and taken the lead on 2. This upcoming year I have the privilege of serving as President, where I will hopefully lead 3 additional shows. Of these 5 shows, 4 have been at Hill Auditorium, a historic venue in Ann Arbor that seats 3,500. Some of the artists that we’ve brought include Rich the Kid, Ceraadi, A R I Z O N A, Electric Guest, Mikky Ekko, Blackbear, Roy Wood$, Jalen Santoy, and A$AP Ferg.


Rich the Kid + Ceraadi

In the fall of 2019, Big Ticket Productions brought Rich the Kid. I co-managed this show, managing 30 people who made the show happen. I secured unique sponsorship opportunities, and was key in the artist decision making process, helping to bring the first ever female artist to any student-led show at Michigan.


Hip Hop Summit featuring Jalen Santoy

During winter of 2018, I spearheaded the partnership between Big Ticket Productions and Hip Hop Congress, another UM Student Organization. I then pitched this opportunity to the Big Ticket Executive board, securing $5,000 in funding for the event. This free event hit capacity in the University’s Museum event space.


Big Ticket Productions 2020 Fall Show

Although this show was postponed from Spring 2020 due to COVID-19, it was already planned and I played an instrumental part in this process. I spearheaded a new way in which we decide which artists we bring, bringing much needed data into the process to help insure financial security and a successful show. After this data collection, I pitched the artist that we ended up deciding on, and I hope that the Fall 2020 show can take place and is a success.
